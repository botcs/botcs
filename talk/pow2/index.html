Machines learning on their own

Abstract

Deep Learning’s true potential lies in its ability to reveal hidden features - yet Supervised learning is driving the industry since DL performance scales up with the amount of training data. What researchers should really focus on is rather designing learning methods that require less training data, or at least as minimal supervising effort from human users as possible.

In this talk several unsupervised methods are reviewed, such as clustering, word embeddings, autoregressive methods (i.e. RNNs), auto-encoders, GANs and their combinations: e.g. Seq-2-seq, Text-2-Image.
