Let's use our brains

Abstract

AI is cool, we have intelligent phones, drones, TVs - even our refrigerator could’ve been intelligent… Many decide to keep their old cellulars, avoid social media and basically refuse any interaction with intelligent systems. Ignoring something this great does not mean it would not surpass us, we will be just left out from the emerging possibilities stuck in the past. The real thing is that unless we take some attempts to understand the key concepts of recent advances in AI, we will be just exposed to tech giants’ intentions. We should ask the right questions and take initiative to prevent exploitation of the advances in AI and more importantly the users of AI.

Exactly this motives this talk, where we discuss the core concepts of AI, study one of its subdomain, Supervised Learning, and revisit the basics of the recently famous Neural Networks. What current methods are good for? What are their downsides and weaknesses? How to improve them?
