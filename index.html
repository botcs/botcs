"What I cannot create, I do not understand"

Feb 24, 2018 6:00 PM — 7:30 PM
Palace of Wonders: AI Workshop

Brain experiments are pretty expensive, or at least unethical but for example we can train a classifier to recognise objects of any arbitrary classes and peek inside the resulting visual cortex without damaging them. The question is: do these neural networks learn the same representations as humans? Definitely not!

What about training them to use the same encoding as the human visual, so we don’t have to crack our skulls to know what is going on inside. Generative methods could help us deciphering our brain’s language, so we can actually see through someone else’s eye…

Are we actually living the golden age of AI breakthroughs?

Machines learning on their own

Nov 9, 2017 6:00 PM — 7:30 PM
Palace of Wonders: AI Workshop

Deep Learning’s true potential lies in its ability to reveal hidden features - yet Supervised learning is driving the industry since DL performance scales up with the amount of training data. What researchers should really focus on is rather designing learning methods that require less training data, or at least as minimal supervising effort from human users as possible.

In this talk several unsupervised methods are reviewed, such as clustering, word embeddings, autoregressive methods (i.e. RNNs), auto-encoders, GANs and their combinations: e.g. Seq-2-seq, Text-2-Image.

Let's use our brains

Oct 13, 2017 6:00 PM — 7:30 PM
Palace of Wonders: AI Workshop

AI is cool, we have intelligent phones, drones, TVs - even our refrigerator could’ve been intelligent… Many decide to keep their old cellulars, avoid social media and basically refuse any interaction with intelligent systems. Ignoring something this great does not mean it would not surpass us, we will be just left out from the emerging possibilities stuck in the past. The real thing is that unless we take some attempts to understand the key concepts of recent advances in AI, we will be just exposed to tech giants’ intentions. We should ask the right questions and take initiative to prevent exploitation of the advances in AI and more importantly the users of AI.

Exactly this motives this talk, where we discuss the core concepts of AI, study one of its subdomain, Supervised Learning, and revisit the basics of the recently famous Neural Networks. What current methods are good for? What are their downsides and weaknesses? How to improve them?
